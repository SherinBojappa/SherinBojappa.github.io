Google Scholar CV

I am a PhD student at the University of Massachusetts, Lowell. I am advised by Dr. Anna Rumshisky. I am broadly interested in understanding and improving the generalization, interpretability, and efficiency of neural language models. My research spans training dynamics, representation learning, and the emergence of complex behavior in large models. I also explore methods for efficient model adaptation, such as parameter-efficient fine-tuning (PEFT), to reduce computational cost while maintaining performance. Through my research, I aim to contribute to the development of more transparent, scalable, and adaptable language technologies.
